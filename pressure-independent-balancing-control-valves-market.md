<p><p>The pressure-independent balancing control valves market is segmented into two main types: threaded and flanged valves. Threaded valves feature screw connections, making them suitable for smaller applications and easier installation in tight spaces. Flanged valves, on the other hand, utilize flat surfaces with bolt holes for securely connecting larger pipes, offering greater strength and stability in high-pressure systems. Both types ensure accurate flow control and system balancing, contributing to energy efficiency and optimal performance in HVAC and building automation systems.</p></p>
