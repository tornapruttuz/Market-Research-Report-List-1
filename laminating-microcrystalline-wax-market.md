<p><p>The Laminating Microcrystalline Wax market is categorized into three types based on melting points: low, middle, and high. Low melting point waxes, typically ranging from 50-65°C, are suitable for applications requiring flexibility and lubricity. Middle melting point waxes, around 65-75°C, offer a balance of hardness and flexibility, making them ideal for various laminating processes. High melting point waxes, above 75°C, are utilized for their exceptional hardness and durability, catering to demanding applications that require strong protective coatings.</p></p>
