<p><p>Nucleic acid extraction and purification reagents are crucial for isolating DNA and RNA from biological samples. The magnetic bead method utilizes tiny beads coated with nucleic acid-binding agents, allowing efficient separation under a magnetic field. The centrifugal column method relies on silica-based membranes in columns to trap nucleic acids during centrifugation, providing high purity. Other methods may include organic extraction or enzyme-based techniques. Each method offers distinct advantages in terms of efficiency, yield, and application suitability within molecular biology.</p></p>
