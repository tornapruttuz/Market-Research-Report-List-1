<p><p>Decentralized Identity Systems categorize into two main markets: non-biometrics and biometrics. Non-biometrics focuses on digital identities created through secure, user-controlled means, such as cryptographic keys or blockchain technologies, enabling individuals to manage their identity without relying on centralized authorities. In contrast, biometrics utilizes unique physical characteristics, like fingerprints or facial recognition, to verify identity. This market emphasizes secure access and authentication, often enhancing user experience but raising privacy concerns due to sensitive personal data involved.</p></p>
